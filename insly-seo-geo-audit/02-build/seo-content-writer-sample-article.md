# What Is MGA Insurance Software? A 2026 Buyer's Guide

**MGA insurance software is a platform that lets a Managing General Agent quote, bind, underwrite, administer policies, manage claims, and report on a book of business in one system — without building core insurance infrastructure from scratch.** It combines a product/rating builder, distribution portals, policy administration, accounting, and claims into a single workflow so an MGA can launch new products in weeks instead of months. (Vendor-stated launch timelines such as Insly's **7–14 days** are platform claims, not industry averages.)

If you run or are launching a Managing General Agent, the software you sit on top of decides how fast you can bring a product to market, how cleanly your accounting reconciles, and how much of your margin disappears into manual work. This guide explains what MGA software is, the capabilities that actually matter, how it differs from generic insurance software, and how to evaluate vendors — including how to judge implementation speed and total cost.

## What is an MGA platform?

A **Managing General Agent (MGA)** is a specialized intermediary that holds delegated authority from one or more insurers (capacity providers) to underwrite, bind, and often handle claims on their behalf. Because an MGA performs functions an insurer would normally do in-house, it needs the same operational backbone — but rented, configurable, and faster to stand up.

An **MGA platform** is that backbone delivered as software. At minimum it provides:

- **A product / rating builder** — define products, rating logic, and rules without custom code.
- **Distribution** — broker portals, agent access, and direct/embedded channels.
- **Policy administration** — the full lifecycle from quote to renewal and mid-term adjustment.
- **Insurance accounting and reporting** — bordereaux, premium reconciliation, and reporting to capacity providers.
- **Claims management** — first notice of loss through settlement.

> **Quotable definition (snippet/AI block):** An MGA platform is the end-to-end software an MGA uses to build products, distribute them, administer policies, manage claims, and report to its capacity providers — replacing separate point systems with one configurable stack.

## What MGA insurance software actually does

The capabilities below map directly to the five pillars Insly's MGA product is organized around (product builder, product distribution, insurance accounting and reporting, claims management, scalability — see `onpage-en-mga-insurance-software.json`).

### 1. Build and rate products fast
A configurable product builder lets underwriters change rating, rules, and forms without a development cycle. This is the single biggest driver of speed-to-market for a new MGA program.

### 2. Distribute through brokers and partners
Broker portals and APIs let your distribution scale without re-keying. Embedded and partner channels are increasingly the difference between a flat book and a growing one.

### 3. Keep accounting accurate and reportable
MGAs live or die on clean money movement: premium, commission splits, and bordereaux owed to capacity providers. Software that reconciles automatically removes the most common source of MGA disputes. Insly states **100% accounting accuracy** as a goal on its insurer page (`onpage-en-insurance-software.json`) — treat vendor accuracy claims as targets to verify in a trial, not guarantees.

### 4. Manage claims in the same system
Handling claims where the policy already lives avoids data silos and gives capacity providers a single source of truth.

### 5. Scale without re-platforming
A modular system lets you add products, lines, and geographies on the same core — so growth doesn't trigger a rebuild.

## MGA software vs. generic insurance software

| | Generic insurance software | MGA-specific platform |
|---|---|---|
| Capacity reporting / bordereaux | Often add-on or manual | Built in |
| Delegated-authority workflows | Rarely modeled | Native |
| Product/rating configurability | Varies | Core capability |
| Time to launch a program | Months | Weeks (vendor-claimed) |
| Commission & multi-party accounting | Limited | First-class |

The takeaway: an MGA *can* run on generic insurance software, but it usually means bolting on the delegated-authority and bordereaux logic that an MGA platform gives you out of the box.

## How to choose MGA insurance software (evaluation checklist)

1. **Speed to launch** — ask for a *named* go-live timeline for a comparable product, in writing. (Vendors like Insly advertise **7–14 days**; verify against your own product complexity.)
2. **Configuration vs. code** — can your team change rating and rules, or do you file a ticket every time?
3. **Accounting & bordereaux fit** — does it reconcile premium and produce the reports your capacity providers require?
4. **Distribution channels** — broker portal, API, embedded — which do you actually need?
5. **Total cost** — understand the pricing model. Insly prices on **system scope, implementation, and GWP transacted** (`onpage-en-pricing.json`), with public entry pricing **from €5,000/month** on the MGA page. Model cost against your projected GWP, not a flat headline number.
6. **Modularity & scale** — can you start narrow and expand without re-platforming?
7. **AI capabilities** — tools like Insly's Insurance Copilot are emerging; judge them on concrete workflow time saved, not the label.

## Frequently asked questions

**What is MGA insurance software?**
It is a platform that lets a Managing General Agent build and rate products, distribute through brokers, administer policies, handle claims, and report to capacity providers in one system instead of stitching together separate tools.

**What is the difference between an MGA and a broker?**
A broker arranges cover for clients; an MGA holds delegated authority from an insurer to underwrite and bind on the insurer's behalf — so an MGA needs underwriting and bordereaux capabilities a broker platform may not have.

**How much does MGA software cost?**
Pricing is usually based on system scope, implementation effort, and the gross written premium (GWP) transacted on the platform rather than a flat per-seat fee. Insly publishes entry pricing **from €5,000/month** (User-provided, from Insly's MGA page); your cost depends on GWP and scope.

**How long does it take to launch on an MGA platform?**
Software configuration can be fast — some vendors advertise **7–14 days** to go live with a product — but full commercial launch also depends on capacity agreements, compliance, and data migration outside the platform.

**Do I need MGA-specific software or will generic insurance software work?**
Generic software can work, but MGA-specific platforms include delegated-authority workflows, multi-party commission accounting, and capacity bordereaux out of the box, which generic systems often require you to build.
